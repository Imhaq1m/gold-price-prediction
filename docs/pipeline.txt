1. PROJECT OVERVIEW
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
This project predicts future Gold Prices (via GLD ETF) using a Deep Learning
approach. The pipeline implements a research-grade architecture combining
Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) networks with Multi-Head Self-Attention mechanisms.

Objective: Predict the next day's gold price based on historical market data
           and technical indicators.
Method:    Walk-Forward Cross Validation (Time-Series)
Performance: R² > 0.99 (Bias Corrected)

BEGINNER-FRIENDLY EXPLANATION:
Think of this project as teaching a computer to predict gold prices the same
way a professional trader would. We give it 11 years of gold market history,
31 different market indicators (like trend, momentum, volatility), and a
neural network that can "remember" patterns and "focus" on important events.
The model learns from the past to predict the future.

3. KEY CONCEPTS EXPLAINED
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

--- WHAT IS GLD AND WHY USE IT? ------------------------------------------------
GLD (SPDR Gold Shares) is an ETF that tracks the gold spot price. It trades on
major exchanges and provides OHLCV data, making it the standard proxy for gold
in ML/quantitative finance research.

Why GLD instead of raw gold spot prices:
- Structured market data: OHLCV + volume reflects real trading behavior.
- Easy data access: yfinance API provides clean daily data without paid APIs.
- Includes market microstructure: Gaps, overnight moves, liquidity effects.
- Reproducible: Anyone can fetch the same data for replication.

--- CHRONOLOGICAL ORDER AND TEMPORAL INTEGRITY ----------------------------------
Time series data must be sorted oldest-to-newest to preserve temporal
dependencies. Violating this introduces two critical bugs:

1. Data Leakage: If future data appears in training, the model "cheats" by
   learning patterns that wouldn't exist at inference time. This inflates
   metrics and causes catastrophic failure on unseen data.

2. Rolling Window Calculations: Technical indicators (SMA, RSI, MACD) use
   rolling windows. If day 100 appears before day 50, the rolling mean
   computes on incorrect data, corrupting all downstream features.

Our pipeline enforces chronological order explicitly, even though yfinance
typically returns sorted data.

--- FEATURE ENGINEERING: FROM RAW OHLCV TO MARKET SIGNALS ----------------------
Raw OHLCV columns only provide point-in-time prices. Feature engineering
transforms these into higher-level representations that capture market
structure:

1. Trend Features (SMA, EMA):
   - SMA(10/20/50) and EMA(10/20) capture moving averages over different
     windows. Crossovers (e.g., SMA10 > SMA50) signal trend changes.
   - These give the model explicit trend context rather than forcing it to
     learn from raw prices.

2. Momentum Features (RSI, MACD):
   - RSI(14): Normalized oscillator [0, 100] measuring overbought/oversold.
   - MACD: Difference between fast and slow EMAs, plus signal line and
     histogram. Captures momentum shifts before price reversals.

3. Volatility Features (Bollinger Bands, Rolling Std):
   - Bollinger Band width measures expansion/contraction of volatility.
   - Rolling standard deviation quantifies local noise.
   - These help the model distinguish stable regimes from turbulent ones.

4. Lag Features:
   - Explicit columns for Close/Returns at t-1, t-2, t-3, t-5, t-10.
   - Provides the model with direct access to recent history without relying
     solely on LSTM memory.

5. Cyclical Features (Day of Week, Month, Quarter):
   - Gold exhibits seasonality (e.g., year-end demand, Asian market hours).
   - Cyclical encoding helps the model learn periodic patterns.

Result: 5 raw columns → 31 features that encode trend, momentum, volatility,
history, and seasonality.

--- TARGET DEFINITION: WHY PREDICT RETURNS, NOT PRICES? ------------------------
Financial price series are non-stationary: the mean and variance change over
time. Gold moved from ~$1,000 (2015) to ~$2,300 (2024). A model trained on
absolute prices will suffer from covariate shift — it learns price levels
specific to the training period and fails to generalize.

Returns are stationary:
  target_return = (Close(t+1) / Close(t)) - 1

Key properties:
- Mean ~0, bounded variance (typically [-10%, +6%] for daily gold returns).
- Distribution is stable across time periods (no trend component).
- The model learns percentage changes, which are transferable regardless of
  the absolute price level.

At inference:
  Predicted_Price = Previous_Close * (1 + Predicted_Return)

This approach is standard in quantitative finance and aligns with how
traders think about positions (% returns) rather than absolute levels.

--- MULTI-HEAD SELF-ATTENTION: MECHANISM AND INTUITION -------------------------
Self-attention computes relationships between all pairs of positions in the
sequence. For our 30-day window, it learns which days are most predictive
of the next day's return.

MATHEMATICAL FORMULATION:
For each attention head h:
  Q_h = X * W_q_h    (Query projection)
  K_h = X * W_k_h    (Key projection)
  V_h = X * W_v_h    (Value projection)

  Attention_h = softmax(Q_h @ K_h^T / sqrt(d_k)) @ V_h

Where:
- X: LSTM output, shape (batch, 30, 50)
- d_k: Key dimension per head = 50 / 4 heads = 12.5 → adjusted to 50
- Scaling factor sqrt(d_k) prevents vanishing gradients in softmax

The 4 heads learn different representation subspaces:
  Head 1 might attend to price shock events (large negative returns).
  Head 2 might attend to volume anomalies (institutional activity).
  Head 3 might attend to recent trend continuity (momentum).
  Head 4 might attend to volatility regime changes.

Outputs from all heads are concatenated and projected:
  MultiHead(Q, K, V) = Concat(Head_1, ..., Head_4) * W_o

RESIDUAL CONNECTION AND LAYER NORM:
  Output = LayerNorm(MultiHead(Q, K, V) + X)

The residual connection (X + attention output) solves two problems:
1. Gradient flow: Gradients can skip through the residual path, preventing
   vanishing gradients in deep networks.
2. Identity preservation: The model can learn to "ignore" attention if it's
   not useful for a particular input, falling back to the LSTM representation.

WHY MULTI-HEAD OVER SINGLE ATTENTION:
A single attention head can only learn one alignment strategy (e.g., "focus
on recent days"). Multi-head attention learns diverse strategies in parallel,
similar to how CNNs use multiple filters to detect different features (edges,
textures, shapes). In financial time series, this allows the model to
simultaneously track short-term shocks, medium-term trends, and long-term
regime stability.


4. DETAILED PIPELINE FLOW
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

--- STEP 1: DATA EXTRACTION (Source: data_module.py) ---------------------------
Input:
  - Ticker: "GLD" (SPDR Gold Shares)
  - Start Date: "2015-01-01"
  - End Date: "Current Date"

Process:
  - Fetch daily financial data via yfinance (Yahoo Finance API).
  - Extract columns: Open, High, Low, Close, Volume, Dividends, Splits.
  - Ensure data covers ~11 years (approx. 2,800 trading days).

Output:
  - Raw DataFrame (Unprocessed OHLCV data).


--- STEP 2: DATA PREPROCESSING (Source: data_module.py) ------------------------
Input:
  - Raw DataFrame from Step 1.

Process:
  - Clean: Handle missing values (NaN) using Forward Fill -> Backward Fill.
  - Sort: Ensure data is chronologically ordered (oldest to newest).
  - Deduplicate: Remove any duplicate timestamps.
  - Index: Set 'Date' as the DataFrame index.

Output:
  - Clean DataFrame (Ready for analysis).


--- STEP 3: FEATURE ENGINEERING (Source: feature_engineering.py) ---------------
Input:
  - Clean DataFrame (OHLCV).

Process:
  - Create 31 Technical Indicators to capture market dynamics:
    1. Trend: SMA (10, 20, 50), EMA (10, 20), Crossovers.
    2. Momentum: RSI (14), MACD, MACD Signal, MACD Histogram.
    3. Volatility: Bollinger Bands (Width, Position), Rolling Std Dev.
    4. Price Action: Log Returns, High-Low Range, Open-Close Range.
    5. Lag Features: Close/Returns lagged by 1, 2, 3, 5, 10 days.
    6. Time Features: Day of Week, Month, Quarter.
  - Drop NaN rows resulting from rolling window calculations.

Output:
  - Feature DataFrame (31 Input Columns).


--- STEP 4: TARGET DEFINITION (Source: main.py) --------------------------------
Input:
  - 'Close' price column from Feature DataFrame.

Process:
  - Convert non-stationary Price data into Stationary Return data.
  - Formula: Target = (Close(t+1) / Close(t)) - 1
  - This represents the percentage change expected for the next day.

Output:
  - Target Column: 'target_return' (Values typically between -10% and +6%).


--- STEP 5: WALK-FORWARD CROSS VALIDATION (Source: main.py) --------------------
Configuration:
  - Folds: 5
  - Initial Train: 70% of data.
  - Window: Expanding (Train set grows by 1 fold each iteration).

  [ Loop 1 to 5 ]:
    |
    |--- A. DATA SPLITTING
    |     Process: Slice DataFrame into Train and Test sets based on time.
    |
    |--- B. FEATURE SCALING (Source: sklearn.preprocessing)
    |     Process: Normalize all features and target to [0, 1] range.
    |     Tool: MinMaxScaler (fitted on Train, transformed on Test).
    |
    |--- C. SEQUENCE CREATION (Source: feature_engineering.py)
    |     Process: Convert tabular data to 3D sequences (Samples, Time, Features).
    |     Config: Sequence Length = 30 days.
    |     Output: X_train (Shape: ~2000, 30, 31), y_train (Shape: ~2000,).
    |
    |--- D. MODEL ARCHITECTURE (Source: lstm_model.py)
    |     Architecture: LSTM-Attention Hybrid
    |
    |     Input shape: (batch_size, 30, 31)
    |       - batch_size: 32 (configurable)
    |       - seq_len: 30 days
    |       - features: 31 technical indicators
    |
    |     Layer 1: LSTM(31 → 50 hidden units)
    |       - Processes sequence step-by-step, maintaining hidden state h_t
    |         and cell state c_t through gating mechanisms (forget, input,
    |         output gates).
    |       - Captures temporal dependencies: trends, momentum, regime shifts.
    |       - Output: (batch, 30, 50) — hidden state at each time step.
    |       - Followed by Dropout(0.2) for regularization.
    |
    |     Layer 2: Multi-Head Self-Attention(4 heads, embed_dim=50)
    |       - Computes pairwise relationships between all 30 time steps.
    |       - Each head learns a different alignment strategy via independent
    |         Q, K, V projections.
    |       - Attention scores: softmax(Q @ K^T / sqrt(d_k)) — measures how
    |         much each time step should attend to every other step.
    |       - Output: (batch, 30, 50) — contextually enriched representations.
    |       - Residual connection: attn_output + lstm_output
    |       - LayerNorm: Normalizes activations for training stability.
    |
    |     Layer 3: Temporal Pooling
    |       - Takes output at the final time step: attn_out[:, -1, :]
    |       - Shape: (batch, 50) — compressed representation of full context.
    |
    |     Layer 4: Dense(50 → 1)
    |       - Linear projection to single scalar output.
    |       - Output: predicted return (e.g., 0.008 = +0.8%).
    |
    |     WHY THIS ARCHITECTURE:
    |       - LSTM captures sequential patterns (what traditional RNNs struggle
    |         with due to vanishing gradients).
    |       - Attention allows the model to weigh specific days differently
    |         rather than treating all history equally.
    |       - Residual connections prevent gradient degradation across layers.
    |       - Dropout(0.2) at multiple points reduces overfitting on noisy
    |         financial data.
    |
    |--- E. MODEL TRAINING
    |     Config:
    |       - Optimizer: Adam (Learning Rate: 0.003).
    |       - Loss: MSE (Mean Squared Error).
    |       - Callbacks: Early Stopping (Patience: 15 epochs), ReduceLROnPlateau.
    |       - Epochs: Max 50 (usually stops early around 20-30).
    |
    |--- F. PREDICTION & METRICS
    |     Process: Generate predictions on Test set (scaled returns).
    |     Metrics: Calculate R², MAE, RMSE for this fold.
    |
    |--- G. SAVE MODEL
    |     Output: Save model weights to 'models/cv_fold_X.pt'.


--- STEP 6: FINAL EVALUATION & BIAS CORRECTION (Source: main.py) ---------------
Input:
  - Best Model (selected by highest R² from Step 5).
  - Test Set Predictions (Scaled Returns).

Process:
  - A. Inverse Transform: Convert predicted returns from [0,1] scale back to %.
  - B. Price Conversion: 
       Formula: Predicted_Price = Previous_Close * (1 + Predicted_Return).
  - C. Bias Correction:
       Issue: Model tends to overpredict by ~$10 (Systematic Bias).
       Fix: Calculate Mean_Error = Mean(Actual - Predicted).
       Adjust: Final_Prediction = Raw_Prediction + Mean_Error.
  - D. Final Metrics: Recalculate R², MAE, RMSE on corrected prices.

Output:
  - Corrected Predictions (Prices in USD).
  - Final Metrics Report (e.g., R² ~0.99, MAE ~$0.34).


--- STEP 7: VISUALIZATION & EXPORT (Source: evaluation.py) ---------------------
Process:
  - A. Time Series Plot: Overlay Actual vs. Predicted prices over time.
  - B. Error Analysis:
       1. Histogram of prediction errors (should be centered at 0).
       2. Scatter plot of Actual vs. Predicted (should follow diagonal).
  - C. Training History: Plot Loss and MAE curves for the best fold.

Output:
  - Files saved to 'results/':
       1. predictions.csv (Date, Actual, Predicted, Error).
       2. predictions_vs_actual.png
       3. error_distribution.png
       4. training_history.png

--- STEP 8: FLASK WEB DASHBOARD (Source: app.py) --------------------------------
The trained model can be interacted with via a Flask web dashboard:

Flask Routes:
  - GET  /         Renders index.html with:
                     - Historical GLD chart (last 3 months, Chart.js)
                     - Auto-run 7-day prediction (ensemble of 5 CV models)
                     - Number input for prediction horizon (1-30 days)
  - POST /predict  Accepts JSON {"days": N}, returns:
                     - JSON array of {day, date, return_pct, price}

Prediction Pipeline (inferene):
  1. Load artifacts (models, scalers, bias correction) once at startup
  2. Fetch recent GLD data (cached for 1 hour to avoid rate limits)
  3. Preprocess and compute 31 features
  4. Run direct multi-step: one forward pass predicts all H=30 returns
  5. Add bootstrap noise:
       - Sample residuals from historical GLD return distribution
       - Add to each predicted return for realistic day-to-day volatility
  6. Convert returns to prices (cumulative product from last close)
  7. Apply bias correction once (not per-step)
  8. Return prediction array to frontend

Frontend (Chart.js, dark theme):
  - Gold line: historical close prices (last 3 months)
  - Orange dashed line: LSTM prediction with bootstrap noise
  - Results table: Day, Date, Return %, Price
  - Dark background with gold accents

Key Design:
  - Ensemble of 5 CV models for robust predictions
  - Bootstrap noise from historical returns (centered at zero)
    ensures some days go up, some go down — realistic jagged trajectory
  - Artifacts loaded at module level (call load_artifacts() on import)
  - Deterministic seed per date (same prediction all day)

6. GLOSSARY AND TECHNICAL REFERENCE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  Term                  | Technical Definition
  ----------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------
  GLD                   | SPDR Gold Shares ETF; tracks gold spot price, provides
                        | OHLCV data. Ticker: GLD.
  ETF                   | Exchange-Traded Fund; trades on exchanges, tracks an
                        | underlying asset or index.
  OHLCV                 | Open, High, Low, Close, Volume; standard candlestick
                        | data format for financial markets.
  Chronological Order   | Temporal ordering from t_0 to t_n; critical for time
                        | series to prevent data leakage and correct rolling
                        | window computations.
  Data Leakage          | Training on information unavailable at inference time;
                        | causes overoptimistic metrics and poor generalization.
  Feature Engineering   | Domain-specific transformations of raw data into
                        | higher-level representations (e.g., RSI from prices).
  Technical Indicators  | Quantitative formulas applied to OHLCV data; categorized
                        | into trend (SMA), momentum (RSI, MACD), volatility
                        | (Bollinger Bands), and cyclical features.
  Stationarity          | Time series property where mean and variance are constant
                        | over time. Required for many ML models to generalize.
  Returns               | Percentage price change: (P_{t+1} / P_t) - 1. Stationary
                        | transformation of non-stationary price data.
  Covariate Shift       | Change in input distribution between train and test sets;
                        | occurs when training on absolute prices across different
                        | eras (e.g., $1000 vs $2300 gold).
  LSTM                  | Long Short-Term Memory; RNN variant with gating
                        | mechanisms (forget, input, output gates) and cell state
                        | to handle long-range dependencies. Solves vanishing
                        | gradient problem of vanilla RNNs.
  Self-Attention        | Mechanism computing pairwise relationships between all
                        | positions in a sequence. Output = softmax(QK^T / sqrt(d))V
  Multi-Head Attention  | Multiple independent attention mechanisms operating in
                        | parallel, each learning different representation subspaces.
  Query/Key/Value (QKV) | Projections of input used in attention: Query (what I'm
                        | looking for), Key (what I contain), Value (my content).
  Residual Connection   | Skip connection: output = f(x) + x. Enables gradient
                        | flow through deep networks and identity preservation.
  Layer Normalization   | Normalizes activations across features (not batches);
                        | stabilizes training, especially with RNNs/Transformers.
  Dropout               | Stochastic regularization; randomly zeroes a fraction
                        | of activations during training. Rate=0.2 means 20%.
  Overfitting           | Model memorizes training noise instead of learning
                        | generalizable patterns; detected when val_loss increases
                        | while train_loss decreases.
  Walk-Forward CV       | Time-series validation with expanding training window;
                        | prevents look-ahead bias and simulates real deployment.
  Early Stopping        | Halts training when validation loss stops improving for
                        | N epochs (patience=15). Prevents overfitting.
  Adam Optimizer        | Adaptive moment estimation; combines RMSProp and
                        | momentum. Default lr=0.003 in this project.
  MSE Loss              | Mean Squared Error: (1/n) * sum(y_pred - y_true)^2;
                        | penalizes large errors more than small ones.
  ReduceLROnPlateau     | LR scheduler; reduces learning rate by factor=0.5 when
                        | val_loss stagnates for 7 epochs. Fine-tunes convergence.
  MinMaxScaler          | Linear normalization: x_scaled = (x - min) / (max - min);
                        | maps features to [0, 1] range. Fit on train, apply to
                        | test to prevent data leakage.
  R² (R-squared)        | 1 - SS_res/SS_tot; proportion of variance explained by
                        | model. 1.0 = perfect, 0.0 = baseline (predict mean).
  MAE                   | Mean Absolute Error: (1/n) * sum(|y_pred - y_true|);
                        | interpretable as average dollar error per prediction.
  RMSE                  | Root Mean Squared Error: sqrt(MSE); more sensitive to
                        | outliers than MAE.
  Bias Correction       | Post-processing shift: y_corrected = y_pred + mean_error;
                        | corrects systematic over/under-prediction observed in
                        | the model.
  Gradient Clipping     | Caps gradient norms (max_norm=1.0) to prevent exploding
                        | gradients during backpropagation through LSTM.
